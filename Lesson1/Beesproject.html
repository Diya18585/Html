Welcome to "All about Bees!"

Bees are insects with six legs and wings. They are commonly known as pollinators because they fly from flower to flower spreading pollen and drinking nectar.

Apperance

Bees are typically hairy, small insects with blaack bodies and brown or yellow markings. Some specieshave red, brown or metallic green/blue. They have 6 legs, 5 eyes and 2 pair of wings.

Food

Bees drink nectar which they get from flowers. The pollen tends to stick to them and when they fly to the next flower the pollen falls off, pollinating the flowers.

Helpful Bees!

Bees are pollinators, helping flowers 'reproduce'. They give honey which is a natural sweetner and Beeswax which is used for making candles and even cosmetics!

Helping Bees

The population of bees is declining alarmingly due to deforestation, pesticide use, etc.

What can we do to hep them? First of all, we must educate others about protecting Bees. We can avoid pesticide, plant lots of flowers, and support beekeepers.
